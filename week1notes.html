Remembering User's Info

Lesson 1, Step 1:

-2013-2014 mobile surpassed desktop in popularity
-We saw dramatic increases in mobile in 2007 because of the iphone and Steve Jobs
-design mobile first because growing in size is easier for you and the user

Lesson 1, Step 2:

1. Be there
2. Be useful
3. Be quick
-Immediate. Relevant. Frictionless.
-Reachability Matters
Speed Matters
-not everyone has the best speed on their network
-reduce image use
-use lower bandwidth for svg/webfonts
-Gzip files to eliminate data that isn't necessary

Lesson 1, Step 3:

-Understanding Matters, most people aren't familiar with the Hamburger icon
-the word "Menu" as a button got 20% more clicks

Lesson 1, Step 4:

-input fields taller, label in field, label float to top
-show the password by default, but give easy method to hide it
-use auto advance for fields like Credit Card info
-when entering numbers is a field, bring up the key pad
-use a link that auto calls the phone number

Lesson 1, Step 5:

-Technology is important but more so how people use it
-Have the title and content at top of screen, small/simple ads, no pop-ups
-use a fullscreen ad underneath content with a scroll window
-stop using pop ups and pop overs
-users don't visit a site to look at ads
-keep it simple and sleek
-make it unobtrusive, content first
